# Camera Shots and Movements Reference

## Shot Types (by framing)

| Shot | Abbreviation | Description | Use |
|-|-|-|-|
| Extreme Wide Shot | EWS | Subject is tiny in the frame, environment dominates | Establishing location, scale |
| Wide Shot | WS | Full body visible, environment context | Scene setting, movement |
| Medium Wide Shot | MWS | Knees up, some environment | Walking, group scenes |
| Medium Shot | MS | Waist up | Dialogue, interaction |
| Medium Close-Up | MCU | Chest up | Emotion with context |
| Close-Up | CU | Face fills frame | Emotion, detail, reaction |
| Extreme Close-Up | ECU | Single feature (eye, hand, object) | Tension, detail emphasis |
| Over-the-Shoulder | OTS | Camera behind one subject looking at another | Conversation, POV |
| Point of View | POV | Camera is the character's eyes | Immersion, discovery |
| Bird's Eye | BE | Directly overhead | Patterns, scale, maps |
| Low Angle | LA | Camera looks up at subject | Power, dominance, awe |
| High Angle | HA | Camera looks down at subject | Vulnerability, overview |
| Dutch Angle | DA | Camera tilted on axis | Unease, tension, style |

## Camera Movements

| Movement | Description | Use |
|-|-|-|
| Static | No movement | Stability, focus, dialogue |
| Pan | Horizontal rotation (left/right) | Reveal, follow action, survey scene |
| Tilt | Vertical rotation (up/down) | Reveal height, follow vertical action |
| Dolly In | Camera moves toward subject | Increasing intimacy or tension |
| Dolly Out | Camera moves away from subject | Reveal context, release tension |
| Truck | Camera moves laterally (left/right) | Follow alongside subject |
| Crane/Boom | Camera moves vertically (up/down) | Reveal, transition, dramatic emphasis |
| Zoom In | Lens zooms (no physical movement) | Draw attention, compress space |
| Zoom Out | Lens widens | Reveal, expand context |
| Tracking Shot | Camera follows moving subject | Action, journey, energy |
| Orbit | Camera circles subject | 360 view, dramatic emphasis |
| Push In | Slow dolly toward subject | Building intensity |
| Pull Back | Slow dolly away | Reveal, context shift |
| Whip Pan | Very fast pan | Transition, energy, surprise |
| Steadicam / Gimbal | Smooth handheld follow | Immersive, intimate movement |
| Handheld | Deliberate shake | Documentary feel, urgency, realism |

## Transitions Between Shots

| Transition | Description | Use |
|-|-|-|
| Cut | Instant switch | Default, fast pacing |
| Dissolve | Gradual blend | Time passage, connection |
| Fade to Black | Image fades out | Scene end, pause |
| Fade from Black | Image fades in | Scene start |
| Wipe | One image pushes another off screen | Stylistic, retro |
| Match Cut | Cut on similar shape/movement | Visual connection between scenes |
| Jump Cut | Cut within same shot, time skip | Energy, montage, disorientation |
| Smash Cut | Abrupt tonal shift | Shock, comedy, contrast |

## Composition Guidelines

- **Rule of Thirds**: Place subjects at intersection points
- **Leading Lines**: Use environmental lines to guide the eye
- **Depth Layers**: Foreground, midground, background for dimension
- **Negative Space**: Empty space creates mood and focus
- **Symmetry**: Centered subjects for formality, power, or surrealism
- **Frame Within Frame**: Doorways, windows, arches for depth

## Common Shot Sequences

### Establishing Sequence
1. EWS of location
2. WS moving closer
3. MS of subject in environment

### Dialogue Sequence
1. MS of both speakers (master shot)
2. OTS of speaker A
3. OTS of speaker B
4. CU for emotional beats

### Action Sequence
1. WS to establish space
2. MS tracking subject
3. CU of key action
4. Reaction shot (CU or MCU)

### Product Reveal
1. ECU of detail/texture
2. Slow dolly out to reveal product
3. Orbit around product
4. WS showing product in context
